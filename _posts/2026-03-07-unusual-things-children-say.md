There are so many funny things that kids say, and I don't know that it's funny
if they're not your kids, but they are my kids. And I love talking to them and
hearing what they think about things.<!--more--> I use that phrase
deliberately.

## Think about things

When my eldest was born, it was Eurovision song contest time and the red-hot
favourite was an Icelandic artist who had a **very** catchy song called "Think
About Things". It's a certified banger, written by a new father saying that he
can't wait to know what his new baby thinks about things.

It hit a chord, and I loved it and listened to it on repeat, vowing to myself
that, all through the long silence of the baby years, I'd always want to know
what my child thought about things.

## Careful what you wish for

My eldest was very slow to speak, and it felt like it took forever. But then
once he started...boy did he start. Now he will not shut up. Even his peers ask
him "Why do you speak so much?"...he just keeps on talking and talking at a
million miles an hour.

In the midst of a ten minute monologue on the different types of dragons on
Berk, I remind myself of how much I used to wish I could hear everything that
my baby thought about.

## This week

This was really all intro to something my eldest said this week that tickled
me:

"I'm really sweaty"
"Why are you so sweaty?"
"There are at least 26 different reasons why I'm sweaty."

Kids say all kinds of delightful and mental things, and it's a wonderful window
onto their bizarre little world. Paying attention and finding the joy in what
they say is one of the best things about parenting.
